## The Problem

**Section Title:** Here's what I keep seeing.

### Card 1: Leadership is frozen
Everyone knows AI matters. Nobody knows where to take the first step. So nothing happens — or worse, everything happens at once with no coordination.

### Card 2: Some people get it. Most don't — yet.
A few people on your team are already using AI to do real work. The rest haven't started — or tried once and bounced off. Without a shared language and shared practice, that gap just keeps growing.

### Card 3: Most AI training fails twice.
It bores the people who are already experimenting and intimidates the people who aren't. A hype-filled demo doesn't help your skeptics, and a cautionary lecture doesn't help your power users. Your team needs something that meets everyone where they actually are.

## Approach

**Label:** My approach

**Title:** Literacy and dexterity together. 

**Intro:** Reading a cookbook doesn't teach you how to be a chef — you have to cook. I teach both: understanding what AI is and isn't, then building the hands-on skill to actually use it well.

### Pillar 1: Build AI Literacy
Develop the ability to understand, navigate, and critically reflect on AI. What is this technology, really? What can it do? What are the actual risks — not the ones in the news, but the ones that affect your work, your team, and the people you serve?

I don't sugarcoat it. AI is built on systems, systems concentrate power, and systems are flawed. Your team deserves to know that ignoring AI doesn't make those problems go away — it just means other people get to shape the tools without you.

### Pillar 2: Practice AI Dexterity
Use AI tools to perform tasks faster, more precisely, and in ways that go beyond your traditional capabilities. This isn't about becoming a prompt engineer. It's about developing a feel for what AI is actually good at versus what it claims to be good at.

Ethan Mollick call this the "Jagged Frontier" — AI capabilities aren't a smooth line. Some things it nails. Some things it confidently gets wrong. The only way to learn the difference is to practice, and I design that practice into every engagement.

## Philosophy

**Label:** What I believe

**Title:** You're not behind.

**Intro:** If you're engaging with AI now, you're exactly where you need to be. Most people still haven't used a chatbot. And the last few years have been navigating a lot of hype. 
    Here are some of my personal rules for using AI that I've developed since experimenting and researching since the release of ChatGPT 3.5 in November 2022.
    

### Law 1
**Heading:** AI should expand your capabilities, not just speed things up.

**Body:** I'm not interested in helping your team write emails faster. I want them doing things they couldn't do before — or doing their best work even better.

### Law 2
**Heading:** AI should not harm people.

**Body:** These tools were built on other people's work, often without their permission. That matters. I teach teams to use AI in ways that respect the humans behind the data.

### Law 3
**Heading:** You can't trust AI companies to get this right for you.

**Body:** Every AI company in the world has their own agenda. It's up to us to take personal responsibility of how to use these tools. I help your team establish frameworks, shared language, and a shared sense of responsibility.
